WCCI 2026 Special Session

Evolutionary and Swarm-Based Optimization in Smart and Sustainable Agriculture

Driving Innovation in Smart Agriculture

Abstract

The profound transformation of the agricultural sector is imperative to meet the dual challenges of ensuring global food security and adhering to environmental sustainability. Smart Agriculture, leveraging IoT, big data, robotics, and advanced sensing, generates complex, high-dimensional data and presents multifaceted optimization problems that are dynamic, multi-objective, and constrained.

This special session provides a dedicated platform for showcasing cutting-edge research at the intersection of computational intelligence and modern agriculture. We invite contributions exploring how evolutionary algorithms and swarm intelligence can deliver robust, adaptive solutions across the agricultural value chain — from precision resource management to resilient supply networks.

Topics of Interest

  • Multi-objective optimization for site-specific irrigation, fertilization, and pesticide application
  • Sensor placement and deployment strategies for optimal field monitoring
  • Yield prediction and spatial data analysis using evolutionary machine learning
  • Path planning and task allocation for autonomous agricultural robots and UAV fleets
  • Evolutionary optimization of robotic harvesting and weeding maneuvers
  • Optimization of harvesting schedules, storage, and logistics under uncertainty
  • Swarm-based models for sustainable agricultural supply chains
  • Feature selection and hyperparameter tuning for forecasting models
  • Evolutionary algorithms for climate-resilient crop planning and genotype selection
  • Constrained optimization for water-energy-food nexus management
  • Swarm-based techniques for monitoring and minimizing carbon footprint
